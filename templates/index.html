Illinois Public Pensions Database

The crisis revolving around public-employee pensions is among the most vexing affecting state government and cities throughout Illinois, with little agreement on how to fix it. The Better Government Association aims to provide clarity on this important topic with our updated Illinois Public Pensions Database.

This updated, by-the-numbers guide to the largest public pension funds...

  • Shows how well Illinois, Cook County, Chicago, the suburbs and some Downstate pensions are collectively funded, as well as breakdowns for each of those individual pension funds.
  • Compares the present costs for paying down old debts versus the projected costs to cover future benefits.
  • Allows users to search for individuals receiving pensions, a complement to our Illinois Public Salaries Database.

Note: If you don’t see funding data, we will be adding it as soon as it is available.

You can also examine how much of a total pension bill goes for benefits earned in the current year versus how much of the bill goes to make up for underpayments in the past.

What are we comparing to tell that story?

  • “Employer normal cost”, which is the cost to taxpayers to cover pension benefits earned in a given year.
  • “Amortization cost”, which is the cost of borrowing to pay back underfunding in the past.
